Kiwanis/ARG Tree Farm

The Kiwanis/ARG Tree Farm is a project that utilizes a 169 acre tract of land to generate revenue to benefit local health care. Since its inception almost fifty years ago, it has enabled to Kiwanis Club to donate over $500,000 to Bradford Regional Medical Center. Contributions have helped to improve pediatric and maternal care, purchase X-ray equipement, and provide startup money for the Upbeat and Cardiac Care programs.



Community Service

Whether it's helping an elderly home-owner with house repairs at Christmas in April, assisting with children's programs at the library, or serving meals to the needy at the Friendship Table, the Bradford Kiwanis Club is active throughout the community.

Emphasis on children and youth is reflected in many of the Bradford Kiwanis Club's annual Christmas Party for the Children at Beacon Light Behavioral Health Systems is a tradition that dates from the founding of the club in 1936. Kiwanis sponsorship of the Key Club at Bradford Area High School dates from 1948.


Community service extends across a broad range of needs. You'll find Kiwanians delivering flowers during the American Cancer Society's Daffodil Days, ringing the bell at the Salvation Army Christmas kettles, volunteering at Special Olympics events, and serving meals at the Friendship table.The Kiwanis Club often lends a hand to help stage civic events, improve parks and playgrounds, and provide volunteers at countless projects.

Kiwanis Kapers

The Kiwanis Club of Bradford is proud of its tradition of combining fun with community service. This is evident in the “Kiwanis Kapers,” the club’s annual variety show fundraiser, which turns 56 this year!


Every year since 1957, members of the club have organized a stage show using local amateur talent. Kiwanians participate on-stage as well as behind the scenes in the club’s only fundraiser.


Over the years, the show has taken many different forms, from musical stage shows to variety. Themes have ranged from Broadway to mystery to parodies of popular entertainment. Whatever the format, Kiwanis Kapers is a much anticipated local tradition with emphasis on family entertainment.


Kapers is a lot of fun, not just for our club, but for the entire community. We are able to showcase all kinds of wonderful talent and provide an enjoyable evening of live entertainment.


Kapers always has strong support from the community, especially patrons and program advertisers, who ensure that our show will be a financial success.


The Bradford Kiwanis Club is know throughout the state for its "Kapers" production with fellow Kiwanians traveling to enjoy the show. In fact, the "Kapers" production is often the topic of discussion at the Kiwanis Mid-Winter and Summer Conferences


Last year’s show, “Working 9 to 5,” featured vocal groups, jazzy dance numbers, singers, a children’s number, high school percussion composition, University of Pittsburgh at Bradford Improvers, and comedy which had the audience roaring with approval. Proceeds from Kiwanis Kapers are used to support a wide range of charitable causes, with special emphasis on children’s programs.


Plans are well underway for the 50th anniversary show, which will feature highlights from past years, along with exciting new material. Why not plan a trip to Bradford, PA, November 9, 10, and 11 to be part of a Kiwanis tradition that has spanned 56 years!
